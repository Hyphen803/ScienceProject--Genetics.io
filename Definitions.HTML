Integral Definitions

Image of a Nucleotide [fig A]

Bits and Trits

A single digit of data in a base 2 or 3 system (either base 3 (trit; 0,1,2) or 2 (bits; 0,1)

Terinary

A number system using base 3 instead of Binary (a number system using base 2)

Read

to interpret the bits or trits back into legible data or useable numbers

Encode

to turn legible data into bits or trits that can be stored in the DNA or drive

Write

to put encoded data into the storage (DNA or drive) in the form of writing on a magnetic disk or tape, setting capacitors, or in the form of writing on a DNA strand using codons.

Bases and Nucleotides

A nucleotide is the building block of all Nucleic acids (DNA, RNA, etc.) formed of a Phosphate, a sugar and a nitrogenous base (the sugar and Phosphate make up the "sugar-phosphate backbone") [Fig A]. The bases are where the information is stored using 4 bases: Adine, Thymine, Guamine, and Cytosine.

Base Pair

Bases naturally pair up; Thymine with Adinine and Cytosine with Guamine [Fig A]which allows them to be read, and properly produce the required amino acids, or in our case trits
